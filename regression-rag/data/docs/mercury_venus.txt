Mercury is the smallest planet in the solar system and the closest to the Sun. It has a diameter of 4,879 kilometers. Mercury completes one orbit around the Sun in 88 Earth days, making it the fastest planet. Its surface temperature swings from -180 degrees Celsius at night to 430 degrees Celsius during the day. Mercury has no moons and no rings. The first spacecraft to visit Mercury was Mariner 10 in 1974. Mercury has a very thin atmosphere called an exosphere, composed mainly of oxygen, sodium, and hydrogen.

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and the hottest planet in the solar system, with a surface temperature of 465 degrees Celsius. It has a diameter of 12,104 kilometers. Venus takes 243 Earth days to rotate once on its axis, making its day longer than its year, which is 225 Earth days. Venus rotates in the opposite direction to most planets — a phenomenon called retrograde rotation. It has no moons. The atmospheric pressure on Venus is 92 times that of Earth. The Magellan spacecraft mapped Venus between 1990 and 1994.
