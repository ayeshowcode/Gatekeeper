Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and the largest planet in the solar system. It has a diameter of 139,820 kilometers. Jupiter takes 11.86 Earth years to orbit the Sun and just 9 hours and 55 minutes to rotate on its axis, making it the fastest-rotating planet. Jupiter has 95 known moons. Its four largest moons — Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto — are called the Galilean moons, discovered by Galileo Galilei in 1610. The Great Red Spot is a storm on Jupiter that has lasted for over 350 years. Jupiter has a faint ring system. The Juno spacecraft entered Jupiter's orbit in July 2016.

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and is most recognized for its prominent ring system. It has a diameter of 116,460 kilometers. Saturn takes 29.5 Earth years to orbit the Sun. Its rings are composed mainly of ice particles and rocky debris, and they extend up to 282,000 kilometers from Saturn's center. Saturn has 146 known moons; the largest is Titan, which has a thick atmosphere and liquid methane lakes. Saturn's density is so low that it would float in water. The Cassini spacecraft orbited Saturn from 2004 to 2017.
