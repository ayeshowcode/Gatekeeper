Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun and rotates on its side, with an axial tilt of 97.77 degrees. It has a diameter of 50,724 kilometers. Uranus takes 84 Earth years to orbit the Sun. It has 27 known moons, named after characters from Shakespeare and Alexander Pope. The five largest moons are Miranda, Ariel, Umbriel, Titania, and Oberon. Uranus has 13 known rings. The average temperature in its upper atmosphere is -224 degrees Celsius, making it the coldest planetary atmosphere in the solar system. Voyager 2 is the only spacecraft to have visited Uranus, flying by in 1986.

Neptune is the eighth and farthest planet from the Sun. It has a diameter of 49,244 kilometers. Neptune takes 165 Earth years to complete one orbit of the Sun. A day on Neptune lasts 16 hours and 6 minutes. Neptune has 16 known moons; the largest is Triton, which orbits in the opposite direction to Neptune's rotation. Neptune has 6 known rings. The average surface temperature is -214 degrees Celsius. The strongest winds in the solar system have been recorded on Neptune, reaching speeds of 2,100 kilometers per hour. Voyager 2 flew past Neptune in 1989 and remains the only spacecraft to have visited it.
