Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known planet to support life. It has a diameter of 12,742 kilometers. Earth takes 365.25 days to orbit the Sun and 24 hours to rotate on its axis. Earth has one moon, simply called the Moon, which has a diameter of 3,474 kilometers. The surface of Earth is 71 percent water. Earth's atmosphere is composed of 78 percent nitrogen and 21 percent oxygen. The highest point on Earth is Mount Everest at 8,849 meters above sea level.

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and is often called the Red Planet because of its reddish iron oxide surface. It has a diameter of 6,779 kilometers. Mars takes 687 Earth days to orbit the Sun. A Martian day, called a sol, is 24 hours and 37 minutes long. Mars has two small moons named Phobos and Deimos. The tallest volcano in the solar system, Olympus Mons, is on Mars and stands 21.9 kilometers high. The average surface temperature on Mars is -60 degrees Celsius. NASA's Perseverance rover landed on Mars on February 18, 2021.
